Education


As a small and strongly community rooted organization in a region, we focus on initiatives that foster health, justice and food and energy resiliency in our own region.


Learn more on this page about our past and present education programs in, with, and for the community.


We ran several food distribution programs in partnership with Quabbin Harvest Community Market: Recipes for Wellness, Senior Farmshare, and Stocking Up Shares for low-income families and elders.


NEW! Women Healing Women. We are committed to creating program groups that are diverse in regards to culture and race, age, and life experience. We make this free opportunity available to low-income women who live in the North Quabbin region who want to enhance wellbeing, learn new things, and who would benefit from time with a supportive, diverse group of women in a relaxing outdoor setting.


SOL(Seeds of Leadership) Garden for North Quabbin teens ran for 20 years and you can find many resources on that site. Contact us about Craft Your Own Life intensives for ages 20-24 as plans for the coming year shape up.


We installed over 50 raised beds through our Grow Food Everywhere for Health and Justice program that provided gardens to families and childcare centers in need of, and unable to afford fresh food, and wellness gardens at central community locations. Follow the link above for many resources.


School Garden workshops and resources can be found on that page.

The North Quabbin Garlic and Arts Festival that we organize with our neighbors, a scent-sational, environmentally innovative event that ignites the cultural, local, economic, and agricultural vitality of our region.
